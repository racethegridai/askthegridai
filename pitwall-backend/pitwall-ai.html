Pirelli Compounds
Hard (C2)
Long-run tyre — low deg
Expected for the long first or second stint. Slower but durable. Teams who qualify on mediums may run hard to the end.
Medium (C3)
All-rounder — main race tyre
Expected to be the most-used compound. Balances pace and longevity. Most teams likely start on medium if starting from the midfield.
Soft (C4)
Qualifying tyre — degrades fast
Everyone qualifies on soft. Soft stints in the race are short — high deg in Miami heat. If safety car comes, expect soft-shod gambles from drivers outside the points.
Race Strategy
Likely: 1-Stop
Soft → Medium/Hard
Optimal without a safety car. Pit around lap 18–22. Mercedes and McLaren with upgrades may manage tyres well enough to go 1-stop.
Safety Car Scenario ⚠
Free pit stop window
Safety car probability is 47%. If it comes before lap 30, expect everyone to pit for free. This is when the race order resets — Hamilton and Norris are the drivers best placed to benefit.
Sprint Weekend Note
Sprint race Saturday
Miami is a sprint weekend. Saturday's sprint (19 laps) gives teams live tyre data before the main race. Teams will use the sprint to understand deg rates on each compound.

Race Day Forecast
Afternoon Thunderstorm Risk
38% chance of rain
Miami races run in the afternoon local time — peak thunderstorm hours in Florida. Rain is not guaranteed but is a genuine possibility. Could arrive mid-race and completely rewrite the strategy.
Track Temperature
45–50°C surface temp
Hot tarmac accelerates tyre degradation on the softer compounds. Teams running the C4 soft will see blistering within 12–15 laps. This drives the 1-stop strategy.
Humidity Effect
High humidity all weekend
South Florida in May is extremely humid. Affects driver hydration and car cooling. Brake temperatures run higher than expected. Some cars historically overheat in Miami traffic.
If Rain Comes
Red Flag Risk ⚠ 24%
Potential race stoppage
Heavy rain on a semi-street circuit can trigger a red flag. In 2024 a brief shower caused a VSC period. If a full red flag comes, teams can change tyres freely — this is the wildcard that could let any driver win.
Drivers who benefit from rain
Hamilton, Norris
Hamilton is historically exceptional in mixed conditions — his wet-weather instinct is unmatched. Norris won here in 2024 partially through excellent tyre management. Both could gain positions if rain arrives.

Title Fight
Antonelli — 72 pts · P1
19-year-old championship leader
The most astonishing story in F1 right now. Kimi Antonelli is 19 years old, in his first season, leading the world championship in a Mercedes. He replaced Hamilton — and is outscoring him. Miami is his to lose. But young drivers under pressure at street circuits with safety cars… this is where championships unravel.
Hamilton — 41 pts · P4
First Miami GP in Ferrari red
Lewis Hamilton moved to Ferrari this season after 12 years at Mercedes. He is 4th in the championship and 31 points off the lead. Miami is a circuit he has driven here many times. Ferrari have upgrades. Hamilton is motivated. This could be the race where he announces himself at his new team.
Challengers
Verstappen — 12 pts · P9
Red Bull engine upgrade
Verstappen has openly compared the 2026 Red Bull to Mario Kart — the homemade Red Bull engine loses power on straights and is unreliable. But Red Bull confirmed an engine upgrade for Miami. If it works, the long back straight here could be his hunting ground. Do not write him off.
Norris — 37 pts · P5
Defending Miami winner
Lando Norris won the 2024 Miami Grand Prix — his first ever F1 victory on this very circuit. McLaren also bring an upgrade package to Miami. He knows this track can make careers. The gap to Antonelli is 35 points — a win plus a bad weekend for Antonelli and the title race is on.
Team Battles
Mercedes vs McLaren
Both bringing aero upgrades
Mercedes have dominated the first 4 races but McLaren are closing. Both teams confirmed upgrade packages for Miami. The development war is heating up — this could be the race where McLaren close the gap to Mercedes on raw pace.

The big 2026 changes
Power Unit
The yo-yo power effect
Cars accelerate then suddenly lose power on straights. The new hybrid deployment rules cause this. Red Bull suffer most.
Aerodynamics
Active aero replaced DRS
DRS is gone. Active aero adjusts wings automatically. Some drivers love it. Most hate it.
Race Starts
Why starts look so weird
Cars rev for 10 seconds before moving. The hybrid system needs time to charge before the clutch releases.
Teams
Two brand new teams
Audi and Cadillac joined F1. Cadillac is the first American team in decades. Miami is their home race.
Scoring
Sprint weekends score more
Miami is a sprint weekend. Saturday results now matter more for the championship than before.
Controversy
Why Verstappen is so angry
He compared 2026 racing to Mario Kart. Red Bull engine loses power on straights. Genuinely furious.
